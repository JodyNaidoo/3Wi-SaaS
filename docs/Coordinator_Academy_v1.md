# MODULE 1 — The Operating Model

## Why we work this way

3Wi sells expertise it cannot afford to hire. Instead of buying senior consultants at R 50k/month each, we:
1. Encode expertise into reusable **workflows** (the templates)
2. Use **AI agents** to execute the repeatable parts cheaply
3. Use **coordinators** (you) to run things in the physical world + handle the parts AI can't
4. Use **Jody** for relationships, strategic judgment, and exception handling

The result: an engagement that would cost a normal consultancy R 300k can be delivered by us at R 150k profitably, because most labour is automated.

## Who does what — the role matrix

| Activity type | Director (Jody) | Coordinator (you) | AI Agent | Client |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Strategic call with client | **Lead** | Take notes | — | Participate |
| Pricing decision | **Lead** | Recommend | — | — |
| Drafting proposal docs | Review | **Lead** + verify | Draft | — |
| Workflow execution (day-to-day) | Hands off | **Lead** | Execute | Receive |
| Reporting (monthly, quarterly) | Sign off | **Lead** assembly | Generate | Receive |
| Client communication (routine) | — | **Lead** | — | Engage |
| Client communication (sensitive) | **Lead** | Brief Jody first | — | Engage |
| Exception / escalation | **Lead** | Detect + flag | — | — |
| Contracting | **Lead** sign | Draft + admin | — | Sign |

**Translation:** You drive most things. AI does the heavy work. Jody appears for strategy, sign-off, and crises.

# MODULE 2 — Decision Trees

These are the most important part of the Academy. Memorise them. Print them. Live by them.

## 2.1 Phase 1 Discovery — Go/No-Go

**Question being answered:** Should we take this client on?

```
   START: Inbound enquiry received
        │
        ▼
   Q1. Is the problem in our wheelhouse?
       (Marketing, Accounting, Capital, Training, HR, Maintenance, IBS Strategy)
        │
        ├── NO  → DECLINE: refer externally if we have a trusted partner;
        │         log as a lost lead in CRM
        │
        └── YES ↓
   Q2. Has the client articulated a budget (even loosely)?
        │
        ├── NO  → SCHEDULE strategic call with Jody first
        │         (he qualifies before coordinator invests time)
        │
        └── YES ↓
   Q3. Is the implied budget within our minimums?
       (Tier C: R 25k+ · Tier B: R 150k+ · Tier A: R 480k/yr+)
        │
        ├── NO  → DECLINE politely; offer a free market-signal conversation
        │         (relationship building, not paid work)
        │
        └── YES ↓
   Q4. Is there future pipeline potential beyond this engagement?
        │
        ├── YES → TIER A track. Escalate to Jody to lead proposal.
        │
        └── NO  ↓
   Q5. Is the scope CLEAR (single defined outcome)?
        │
        ├── YES → TIER B track. Coordinator drafts proposal.
        │
        └── NO  ↓
   Q6. Is it a quick foot-in-door / cash-flow accelerator?
        │
        ├── YES → TIER C track. Coordinator drafts proposal.
        │
        └── NO  → ESCALATE to Jody (unclear positioning, needs strategic call)
```

**Rework path:** If Phase 2 reveals wheelhouse mis-fit, return here and either re-tier or terminate with a change-order discussion.

## 2.2 Phase 2 Proposal — Tier Selection + Rework

**Question being answered:** Which pricing tier and what gets in the proposal?

```
   START: Tier identified in Phase 1
        │
        ▼
   For TIER A (Strategic Partner):
       · Jody leads the proposal
       · Coordinator assembles workstream list from Rainmaker catalogue
       · Coordinator produces cost budget + resource plan
       · Jody finalises and presents
       · Outcome share or retainer terms — Jody decides
        │
   For TIER B (Fixed Engagement):
       · Coordinator drafts entire proposal from template
       · Pricing: 30% deposit / 40% midway milestone / 30% on delivery
       · Scope creep = change order (new fee, NOT free work)
       · Jody reviews + signs
        │
   For TIER C (Tactical Sprint):
       · Coordinator drafts proposal in <2 days
       · Pricing: Fixed fee, payable on completion
       · Productised deliverable from our catalogue
       · Jody acknowledges (lightweight review)
        │
        ▼
   REWORK PATHS:
   · Client rejects scope → reduce or walk away
   · Client expands scope → change order (no free expansion)
   · Client demands lower price → check if Tier downgrade fits;
     otherwise walk away unless strategic
   · Client wants different timeline → check capacity; escalate if conflict
```

## 2.3 Phase 3 Contracting — Sign Authority + Escalation

**Question being answered:** Who can sign what?

```
   Q. What is the engagement value?
        │
        ├── < R 25k     → Coordinator can issue + sign (with Jody acknowledgment in SaaS)
        │
        ├── R 25k–R 100k → Coordinator drafts + Jody signs (Jody review takes <24hr)
        │
        ├── R 100k–R 500k → Jody must sign in person (calendar)
        │
        └── > R 500k    → Jody signs + external legal review required
                          (template clauses checked against new clauses)

   ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   ESCALATION TRIGGERS (regardless of value):
   · Unusual liability or indemnity clauses → Jody + legal
   · IP transfer clauses → Jody + legal
   · Conditional / outcome-based payment → Jody decides
   · Client wants payment in instalments different from our standard → Jody
   · Multi-year commitment > 12 months → Jody
```

## 2.4 Phase 4.1 Inception — Report Quality Gate

**Question being answered:** Is the inception report ready to send?

```
   START: Inception report drafted (mostly by AI agent + coordinator finish)
        │
        ▼
   GATE 1: Completeness check
       Are all sections filled?
        │
        ├── NO  → Pause; complete missing sections OR mark "TBD by [date]"
        │
        └── YES ↓
   GATE 2: Accuracy check
       Do all named stakeholders, dates, dollar figures match the contract?
        │
        ├── NO  → REWORK; coordinator fixes
        │
        └── YES ↓
   GATE 3: Client review window
       Has the client PSC member had at least 48hr to review draft?
        │
        ├── NO  → SEND DRAFT for review; wait 48hr
        │
        └── YES ↓
   GATE 4: Open issues
       Are there unresolved client questions or known risks?
        │
        ├── YES → Add to Risk Register; flag in report; SEND
        │
        └── NO  → APPROVE; coordinator can publish + send
                  (no Jody signoff needed unless Tier A)
```

## 2.5 Phase 4.2 Coordination — Escalation Triage

**Question being answered:** Is this issue something I should handle or escalate?

```
   START: An issue, request, or unexpected event arises
        │
        ▼
   Q1. What is the nature of the issue?
        │
        ├── ROUTINE OPERATIONAL (missed deadline, file conflict, calendar clash)
        │      → Coordinator handles; log in SaaS notes
        │
        ├── TECHNICAL/SPECIALIST (need a specific agent, complex calculation)
        │      → Coordinator runs agent; verifies output; uses; logs
        │
        ├── CLIENT RELATIONSHIP (delay, complaint, change of contact)
        │      → Coordinator responds within 4hr; CC Jody on response
        │
        ├── STRATEGIC (scope shift, future pipeline opportunity, competitive intel)
        │      → ESCALATE to Jody same-day
        │
        ├── POLITICAL (internal client tensions, board issues, regulatory)
        │      → ESCALATE to Jody immediately (within 2hr)
        │
        ├── FINANCIAL (payment issue, budget overrun, fee dispute)
        │      → ESCALATE to Jody same-day
        │
        ├── LEGAL (contract dispute, IP issue, liability event)
        │      → ESCALATE to Jody immediately + Affinity Accounting
        │
        └── REPUTATIONAL (PR risk, social media issue, client complaint going public)
               → ESCALATE to Jody immediately (within 30 min)

   ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   "WHEN IN DOUBT, ESCALATE."
   The cost of an unnecessary escalation (Jody loses 5 min) is far less
   than the cost of a missed one (we lose a client).
```

## 2.6 Phase 4.3 Reporting — Publish vs Hold

**Question being answered:** Is this report ready to go to the client?

```
   START: AI agent produced the monthly/quarterly/incident report draft
        │
        ▼
   GATE 1: Does the report match the engagement scope?
       (Are we reporting on the right KPIs the client signed up for?)
        │
        ├── NO  → REWORK; align with scope
        │
        └── YES ↓
   GATE 2: Are the numbers correct?
       (Spot-check at least 3 figures against source data)
        │
        ├── NO  → REWORK; flag to Jody if systematic
        │
        └── YES ↓
   GATE 3: Is the narrative clear?
       (Could a client director understand the executive summary in 60sec?)
        │
        ├── NO  → REWORK the summary; agent can redo
        │
        └── YES ↓
   GATE 4: Are there material changes from the previous report?
       (Big swings, new risks, unexpected results)
        │
        ├── YES → Flag for Jody review BEFORE sending; even on Tier C
        │
        └── NO  ↓
   GATE 5: Does this tier require Jody signoff?
       (Tier A: yes always · Tier B: at quarterly only · Tier C: no)
        │
        ├── YES → Status = "awaiting_review"; queued for Jody
        │
        └── NO  → SEND to client
```

# MODULE 3 — Agent Handoff Patterns

The AI agents (Knockout Marketing, Affinity Accounting, etc.) are powerful but not omniscient. Your job is to verify what they produce before it leaves the building.

## When to trust agent output

| Trust level | Output type | Verification needed |
|---|---|---|
| **High** — usually send as-is | Internal drafts, brainstorm lists, formatting work | Skim for obvious errors |
| **Medium** — verify then send | Reports based on structured data, summaries of meetings | Spot-check 3 facts against source |
| **Low** — always verify with Jody | Client-facing strategic recommendations, financial models, legal-adjacent content, anything with named individuals | Jody reviews before sending |
| **Zero** — never auto-send | Pricing decisions, contract terms, regulatory submissions | Coordinator + Jody both sign-off |

## Verification process before sending to client

For any client-facing artifact:
1. **Does the data exist** in the source system? (Check the underlying invoice, calendar, etc.)
2. **Do at least 3 named entities check out?** (real client names, real dates, real values)
3. **Is the tone right?** (matches our brand voice — not too casual, not too jargon-heavy)
4. **Are there any "hallucinations"?** (Made-up sources, fabricated citations, plausible-sounding nonsense)
5. **Does it match the engagement scope?** (Not over- or under-promising)

If any of these fails → coordinator fixes OR escalates to Jody. Never send an unverified agent output to a client.

## What to do when agent runs out of context

Sometimes the agent says "I don't have enough information to complete this" or produces something that's clearly missing parts. When this happens:

1. **Read the agent's last response carefully** — it usually says WHY it's stuck
2. **Provide the missing context** if you have it (look in SaaS, in client docs, etc.)
3. **Re-run the agent** with the additional context
4. **If still stuck after 2 retries** → flag for Jody; don't try to fudge it yourself

## "Flag for Jody" — when to use (and when not to)

In the SaaS, every activity has a **"Flag for Jody"** button. Use it when:

✅ **USE IT WHEN:**
- You followed the decision tree but the answer is unclear
- The client did something unexpected
- You're about to send something that feels "off" but you can't say why
- Multiple options are valid and you can't pick
- Anything in Phase 4.2 marked ESCALATE above
- You're new and just want a second pair of eyes (Jody encourages this in weeks 1-4)

❌ **DON'T USE IT FOR:**
- Things you can look up in the playbook or this Academy
- Routine status updates ("just FYI")
- Asking Jody for permission to do things you're already empowered to do
- Multiple flags per day (batch them — daily summary > flood)

**Norm:** Jody reviews the queue once per day. If you flag, you'll get a response within 24 hours (often faster). Don't expect instant.

# MODULE 4 — The 12-week Apprenticeship

This is your training arc from "new hire" to "running engagements solo."

## Week-by-week

### Weeks 1-2: SHADOW
- Observe Jody on active engagements (Tier A or Tier B in flight)
- Keep a **Decisions Log**: every non-obvious decision Jody makes, write down what he decided + your interpretation of WHY
- Read this Academy + the IBS Engagement Playbook end-to-end
- Take the [Competence Quiz](../apps/web/public/coordinator-competence-quiz.html) — pass 80% to advance
- **Output:** Decisions Log + quiz pass

### Weeks 3-4: ASSIST
- Draft documents from SaaS templates (proposal sections, inception reports)
- Jody reviews + edits + explains edits (the EXPLANATION is the teaching)
- You do not yet send things on your own
- Deliverables go out with Jody's name
- **Output:** 5+ drafts edited; 1 SaaS template you understand inside-out

### Weeks 5-8: LEAD WITH REVIEW
- You run a SMALL **Tier C** engagement end-to-end
- Jody reviews at gates: end of each phase
- You can send small things; Jody signs off on big things
- You learn to spot escalation triggers
- **Output:** 1 completed Tier C engagement; sign-off from Jody on quality

### Weeks 9-12: LEAD SOLO (with escalation hotline)
- You run a **Tier B** engagement
- Jody is chair of governance only (PSC + exceptions)
- You handle all phases day-to-day
- You use the "Flag for Jody" button as needed
- **Output:** 1 Tier B engagement nearing completion; certified by Jody

### Month 4+
- Running 2 Tier B's concurrently OR 1 Tier A solo
- Mentoring the NEXT new coordinator
- Contributing improvements back to the playbook + Academy

## Sign-off milestones

| Milestone | Gate | Sign-off by |
|---|---|---|
| Quiz passed | End of Week 2 | Self-attested + SaaS records |
| Shadow log reviewed | End of Week 2 | Jody — 30 min review session |
| First draft approved | End of Week 4 | Jody — written feedback |
| Tier C delivered | End of Week 8 | Jody + the client (NPS-style) |
| Tier B nearing close | End of Week 12 | Jody — formal certification |
| Certified Coordinator badge | Week 12+ | Jody — issued in SaaS profile |

# MODULE 6 — Assessment + Certification

## Competence Quiz

Before starting the apprenticeship, take the [Coordinator Competence Quiz](../apps/web/public/coordinator-competence-quiz.html). It's a scenario-based assessment covering all 6 modules.

- **Format:** 12 scenarios, multiple-choice
- **Pass mark:** 10/12 (83%)
- **Time:** ~30 minutes
- **Retakes:** Yes — review wrong answers, study the relevant module, retake
- **Result sharing:** Email your score to Jody when you pass

The quiz is the gate to start the 12-week apprenticeship.

## Apprenticeship sign-off

At the end of Week 12, Jody completes a sign-off form (in SaaS — TBD):
- Quality of work (1-5)
- Independence (1-5)
- Decision-making (1-5)
- Client-readiness (1-5)
- Recommendation: Certify / Extend apprenticeship by 4 weeks / Don't certify

Pass = Certified Coordinator badge in your SaaS profile.

## Certified Coordinator badge

- Visible to Jody and to clients (on engagement teams)
- Required to LEAD a Tier B or Tier A engagement
- Reviewed annually
- Revocable for serious quality lapses (rare — usually means apprenticeship was rushed)

## Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Agent** | An AI workflow (e.g. Knockout Marketing, Affinity Accounting) that produces a specific kind of output |

| **Tier A/B/C** | Engagement pricing categories (Strategic / Fixed / Sprint) |
| **Wheelhouse** | The 6 functional areas where we deliver: Marketing, Accounting, Capital, Training, HR, Maintenance, + IBS Strategy |
| **Workflow Template** | A reusable engagement recipe (e.g. "IBS Engagement v1") |
| **Workflow Instance** | One running execution of a template (e.g. "Hempire ECRDA 2026/2027") |
